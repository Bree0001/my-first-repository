Coding Chronicles: From Uni Dropout to Tech Wizard

Once upon a time in the land of academia, I found myself face-to-face with the daunting reality that university wasn't quite the perfect fit for me. The lecture halls felt like mazes, and every minute spent in the school compound drained my soul. The only thing I ever felt like made sense for me in uni was hanging out with my friends. If you’ve been to CUEA, you definitely know the good old Bogani Thursdays and then hit 1824 thereafter or westmall for some fire pork.

Amidst the chaos of dropping out, enters a silver-lining, my ex-boyfriend, who is as tech-savvy as a robot but has a heart that sometimes needs a reboot. He convinces me that coding is the key to unlocking a world of possibilities, a digital Narnia, if you will. Little did I know that my journey into the tech realm will become both my escape and my comedic adventure.

After our breakup, my heart was shattered into more pieces than a jigsaw puzzle in a tornado. I wallowed in self-pity, and spent the better part of Jamuhuri day (this was two days after the break-up) crying. But then I remembered the wise words of a software update: life goes on.

In the post-breakup era, I found solace in the comforting glow of my computer screen, where coding became my therapy and debugging my catharsis. I began to see the humor in my coding adventures, like the time I accidentally created a program that thinks 2 + 2 equals fish. Classic mix-up, really.

As I embrace my newfound passion, I realize that my journey is just beginning. The thrill of discovery and the promise of a future filled with possibilities fuel my enthusiasm. Sure, I'm still in the shallow end of coding, splashing around like a penguin trying to swim, but the deep waters of algorithms and data structures are beckoning me.

After the breakup, I found myself adrift in the vast ocean of the tech world, with no familiar faces in sight. My only companions were YouTube tutorials, Udemy courses, and the reliable W3Schools. It was a lonely journey, but the virtual mentors on the screen became my guiding lights.

Soon, I stumbled upon a tech mentor who saw potential in my coding endeavors. He encouraged me to post my first repository, but my imposter syndrome kicked in, and I declined. I insisted that my first code was nothing short of mediocre, but he wisely mentioned that everyone starts from somewhere. Undeterred, he continued to push me, like a digital Yoda guiding a fledgling Jedi through the coding galaxy. I promised him to make something better and push it to github and alas, here we are!.

So yeah, that's just the overview of a dropout turned coder, fueled by Fanta, armed with a keyboard, and ready to conquer the tech universe one goofy line of code at a time. The future is uncertain, but it's also a canvas waiting for my algorithmic brushstrokes. And as I type away, I can't help but wonder what other absurd and delightful surprises the tech world has in store for me.

As we delved into the realm of programming, our relationship started resembling a spaghetti code – messy, convoluted, and filled with unexpected bugs. One day, he throws an error message at me that read, "It's not you; it's me," and that's when I knew the relationship was ready for a force quit.

Wait, where are my manners? I forgot to introduce myself.
